Technical Deep Dive

I'm a systems-driven Software Engineer and Data Scientist with roots in neuroscience and a track record of building AI-first pipelines across healthcare, brain research, and financial automation. As an NIH-funded researcher, I've led multi-year neuroimaging studies across Emory, the NIH, and the VA, developing terminal-native pipelines for over 30TB of fMRI, MRS, and TMS data to decode aging, neuroplasticity, and GABA modulation during task learning.

I architected wearable-integrated studies involving 100+ Apple Watches to correlate cardiovascular dynamics with real-time metabolic neurochemistry, bridging physiological telemetry with spectroscopic brain signals for novel insights into human learning and performance.

In digital pathology, I digitized 100,000+ paraffin-embedded tissue blocks and built a secure, cloud-ready archival system using Leica Cerebro and SQL-powered access controls. I onboarded hundreds of researchers across hospital, academic, and compliance boundaries.

Most recently, I served as the sole engineer behind a financial automation stack that unified lead generation, ETL, NLP classification, and PowerBI dashboards. This reduced manual workflows from hours to seconds and drove $1M per day in deal flows.

Whether processing brainwaves, tissue slides, or business logic, I build real-time, reproducible systems that scale. I turn raw data into decisions, and decisions into momentum for teams tackling the world's most complex problems.
